Hunt Summary

We hypothesized that an adversary using DNS as a covert C2 channel would generate abnormal TXT/NULL query volume, high-entropy subdomains, and regular beacon timing to a small set of destinations. Over a 14-day window across 4,200 internal hosts, exactly one host — WKSTN-2207 — exhibited all three signatures against the newly-registered domain cdn-sync-update[.]net: 92% TXT queries, mean subdomain entropy 4.1, and a 60-second ±2s beacon. No other host matched any leg of the signature. Treat WKSTN-2207 as compromised pending endpoint confirmation; the analytic that found it should become a standing detection.

Hypothesis and Scope

Hypothesis. An implant using DNS tunneling for C2 or exfiltration will, on at least one internal host, produce a query profile that diverges from that host's peers on three axes at once: query-type mix (TXT/NULL-heavy), subdomain randomness (high Shannon entropy), and timing (low-variance beacon interval). A host matching all three against an unfamiliar domain is the lead worth confirming.

Techniques targeted. T1071.004 Application Layer Protocol: DNS · T1048.003 Exfiltration Over Unencrypted Non-C2 Protocol.

In scope: all hosts visible to monitored sensors over the 14-day window; DNS A/AAAA/TXT/NULL/CNAME records and per-device query metrics. Out of scope: DoH/DoT to external resolvers (does not transit internal DNS), the guest VLAN and lab segment (unsensed), and query payload content.

Coverage and Limits

A "clean" result only covers what was actually hunted. This hunt reached ~88% of the host population; the guest VLAN and lab segment are not sensed and were not examined.

Hunted and clean

  • 4,199 in-scope hosts showed no TXT/NULL outlier, no high-entropy beaconing destination (E1).
  • No other host queried cdn-sync-update[.]net in the window.

Could not determine

  • Whether data was successfully exfiltrated, and what — query payload content was not decoded.
  • Initial access vector and dwell time before the window.

What ExtraHop can't see

  • DoH/DoT resolving to external servers without transiting internal DNS.
  • The on-host process that generated the queries — endpoint (EDR) territory.
  • The guest VLAN and lab segment, which no sensor observes.
